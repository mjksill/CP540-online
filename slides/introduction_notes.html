Hello, my name is Dr Leo Lue, and welcome to CP540: Project planning, management and methods. You might remember me from the tutorial sessions for CP213.

This lecture will give an overview of the module aims and contents. Its aim is to provide you an overview of

and demonstrate various transferable skills (e.g., note taking, writing, visualization of data, etc.)

and provide some examples of software tools that can help you apply and exercise those skills. It is not meant to dictate the methods and tools that you should use, but rather it is to show you what is available and what you can do with them, their strengths and weaknesses, etc.

This is partly because technology is changing quite rapidly, and the way that we work as an engineer today is not the same as what we did ten or twenty years ago. Because things are rapidly developing and changing so quickly, standards have not had a chance to develop.

So, I am just going to show you what is out there. It may be that in a couple years, what I show you may be obsolete, but this is the environment that we are working in. However, I will try to offer you strategies to try to "future proof" yourselves and mitigate against this.

There are three components of assessment for this module: a project plan for your activities in this academic year, which is worth 20% of the module marks, an exercise on the processing, analysis, and visual presentation of data, which is worth 30% of the module marks, and a literature review on a topic based on sustainable development.
