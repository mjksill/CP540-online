The environment in which we are working in, and in which we will be working in the near future, is rapidly changing. This is partly because technology is changing quite rapidly, and the way that we work as an engineer today is not the same as what we did ten or twenty years ago. Because things are rapidly developing and changing so quickly, standards and expectations have not had a chance to develop. Part of what we will be doing is introducing some of these new tools to you.

and provide some examples of software tools that can help you apply and exercise those skills. It is not meant to dictate the methods and tools that you should use, but rather it is to show you what is available and what you can do with them, their strengths and weaknesses, etc.

There are three components of assessment for this module: a project plan for your activities in this academic year, which is worth 20% of the module marks, an exercise on the processing, analysis, and visual presentation of data, which is worth 30% of the module marks, and a literature review on a topic based on sustainable development.
